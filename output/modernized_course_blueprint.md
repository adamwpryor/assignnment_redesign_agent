# Modernized Syllabus: Introduction to Ethics (PHIL-101)

## Course Description

This course introduces students to the fundamental theories of ethics, including utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics. Students will apply these theories to modern moral dilemmas, focusing on verifiable processes, local context integration, and collaborative critical thinking rather than unverified text generation.

## AI-as-CoPilot Policy

Midwestern University standard: Level 2. Students may use AI as a Co-Pilot to summarize readings or generate initial outlines. However, students must cite specific prompts used and attach an appendix showing how they verified the AI's output against textbook claims. Submitting raw AI output as a final human deliverable is an academic integrity violation.

## Grading Breakdown

* Visual Framework Defenses (replaces Discussions): 30%
* Midterm Annotated Bibliography & Defense: 30%
* Final Local Policy ADR (Architectural Decision Record): 40%

## Assignment 1: Live Decision Tree Defense (Week 3)

### Topic: Utilitarianism vs. Deontology in Local Context

### Instructions

1. Use an AI tool to help you map the core differences between Bentham and Kant regarding the morality of the **Midwestern University Lot C Parking Shortage**.
2. Go to [Mermaid Live Editor](https://mermaid.live). Copy and paste the baseline decision tree provided below into the editor.
3. **Your Task:** Modify the decision tree. Add at least three new branching decision points representing different student stakeholders attempting to park in Lot C. Apply Kantian logic to one branch and Utilitarian logic to another.
4. **Deliverable:** Submit a 3-minute screen recording (via Loom or Canvas Studio) where you walk through your modified decision tree, explaining *why* you structured the outcomes the way you did based on the assigned readings.

*Baseline structure provided in Canvas.*

## Assignment 2: Midterm Annotated Bibliography & Defense (Week 8)

### Topic: The Ethics of Self-Driving Cars

### Instructions

Instead of writing a standard essay on the Trolley Problem, you will build the raw materials for one and defend your curation.

1. Curate a list of 5 peer-reviewed sources regarding algorithmic bias in autonomous vehicles.
2. For each source, write a 3-sentence annotation: The claim, the methodology, and how it connects to virtue ethics. (You may use AI to summarize the articles, provided you include your prompt log).
3. **Deliverable:** Schedule a 5-minute live Zoom or Office Hours defense where I will randomly select one of your 5 articles and ask you to explain its core methodology without looking at your notes.

## Assignment 3: Hyper-Local Policy Evaluation (Week 15)

### Topic: Mass Data Collection on Campus

### Instructions

1. We are looking at the ethics of mass data collection. Review the recent proposal (provided in this week's module) to install automated license plate readers in all Midwestern University parking lots to address the Lot C shortage.
2. Form a group of three. Conduct a 10-minute structured dialogue with your group members regarding how this proposal impacts non-traditional or working adult learners (who make up 40% of our demographic).
3. **Deliverable:** You are submitting an **Architectural Decision Record (ADR)** addressed to the Campus Security Committee. It must follow the strict 1-page ADR formatting constraints provided in the syllabus. It must explicitly cite arguments made by your specific group members during your dialogue, and it must include an appendix showing exactly what prompts you used if you queried an AI to help structure your ADR (per the Level 2 AI-as-CoPilot policy).
