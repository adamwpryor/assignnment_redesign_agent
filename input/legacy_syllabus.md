# Legacy Syllabus: Introduction to Ethics (PHIL-101)

## Course Description

This course introduces students to the fundamental theories of ethics, including utilitarianism, deontology, and virtue ethics. Students will apply these theories to modern moral dilemmas.

## Grading Breakdown

* Discussion Boards: 30%
* Midterm Essay: 30%
* Final Research Paper: 40%

---

## Assignment 1: Weekly Discussion Post (Week 3)

**Topic: Utilitarianism vs. Deontology**

**Instructions:**
Read chapters 4 and 5 of the textbook. Write a 300-word post comparing and contrasting Bentham's Utilitarianism with Kant's Categorical Imperative. Describe a hypothetical scenario where the two theories would produce different moral outcomes. You must reply to at least two classmates' posts with a minimum of 100 words each.

---

## Assignment 2: Midterm Essay (Week 8)

**Topic: The Ethics of Self-Driving Cars**

**Instructions:**
Write a 5-paragraph essay (1500 words) discussing the "Trolley Problem" as it applies to autonomous vehicles. Should a self-driving car be programmed to minimize overall harm (utilitarian approach) or protect its passengers at all costs? Use at least three peer-reviewed sources from the university library database in APA format.

**Rubric:**

* Thesis clarity: 20 points
* Application of ethical theory: 30 points
* Grammar and syntax: 20 points
* Use of sources/APA format: 30 points

---

## Assignment 3: Final Research Paper (Week 15)

**Topic: Technology and Privacy**

**Instructions:**
Write a 10-page research paper exploring the ethical implications of mass surveillance and data collection by social media companies. Argue whether the benefits of targeted advertising outweigh the loss of individual privacy. You must include an introduction, a literature review, your argument, counterarguments, and a conclusion.

*Deliverable:* A Word Document or PDF submitted via the LMS by 11:59 PM on Friday.
